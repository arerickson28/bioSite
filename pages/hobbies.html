Dillon is guarenteed to burst out in song at least twice within a ten minute conversation. Nicknamed "the jukebox", you never know which sentence you say may remind him of a song lyric. Also, be sure to ask him about his Nick Jonas impression!

Dillon fills his time indulging in a slew of creative interests. He is a movie buff and pop culture expert and hosts podcast entitled His Only Vice in which he discusses a film with a different friend each episode.

Despite being a goodball, Dillon takes the craft of acting quite seriously. He has an in-depth understanding of the theory behind good performance and will remind you, "no one wants to see a charcter being sad, that's boring. A character dealing with saddness, on the other hand, is interesting."

Dillon is constantly exploring different avenues of creative expression. Such avenues include artsy photography as pictured on the left and writing comedy screenplays. Whatever it is, Dillon has a knack for making the mundane extraordinary!

Staring as the titular character "the nerd" in a production of The Nerd, Dillon had the opportunity to let his silliness shine. He -- commented "I was basically playing myself." In one brilliant scene, Dillon's character was supposed to appear to be making up a game on the spot. To appear authentic, Dillon memorized no lines for this scene and actually made up the game during the scene each production.
